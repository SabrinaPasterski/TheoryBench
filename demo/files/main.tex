\section{Asymptotics and the celestial sphere}

Asymptotically flat metrics near $\mathscr{I}^+$ in Bondi gauge
$(u,r,z,\bar z)$, with $u=t-r$ retarded time and $(z,\bar z)$
stereographic coordinates on the sphere, admit the large-$r$ expansion
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\dd s^2 = {}& -\dd u^2 - 2\,\dd u\,\dd r
  + 2 r^2 \gamma_{z\bar z}\,\dd z\,\dd\bar z \\
& + \frac{2 m_B}{r}\,\dd u^2
  + r C_{zz}\,\dd z^2 + r C_{\bar z\bar z}\,\dd\bar z^2
  + D^z C_{zz}\,\dd u\,\dd z + D^{\bar z} C_{\bar z\bar z}\,\dd u\,\dd\bar z
  + \cdots,
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:bondi}
\end{equation}
where $\gamma_{z\bar z} = 2/(1+z\bar z)^2$ is the round metric, $m_B$
the Bondi mass aspect, and $C_{zz}$ the free radiative data with news
$N_{zz} = \partial_u C_{zz}$. The BMS group preserves this falloff:
supertranslations $u \to u + f(z,\bar z)$ shift $C_{zz}$ by
$-2 D_z^2 f$, so the vacuum is infinitely degenerate. Bulk scattering
data organize naturally on the $(z,\bar z)$ sphere at fixed null
direction --- the celestial sphere --- where Lorentz transformations act
as global conformal transformations $z \to (az+b)/(cz+d)$.

\section{From soft theorems to currents}

Weinberg's soft graviton theorem, for an $(n{+}1)$-point amplitude with
one graviton of momentum $q = \omega \hat q$ taken soft (all momenta
outgoing),
\begin{equation}
\lim_{\omega \to 0}\, \omega\, \calM_{n+1}\big(\omega\hat q;\{p_k\}\big)
 = \frac{\kappa}{2} \sum_{k=1}^{n}
   \frac{\varepsilon_{\mu\nu}\, p_k^\mu p_k^\nu}{\hat q \cdot p_k}\,
   \calM_n\big(\{p_k\}\big),
 \qquad \kappa^2 = 32\pi G,
\label{eq:soft}
\end{equation}
is the Ward identity of supertranslations:
$\bra{\mathrm{out}} \big( Q_f^+\, S - S\, Q_f^- \big) \ket{\mathrm{in}} = 0$,
with $Q_f = Q_f^{\rm soft} + Q_f^{\rm hard}$. The soft piece is linear
in the zero mode of the news and creates a Goldstone of spontaneously
broken supertranslations; the hard piece reproduces the right-hand side
of \eqref{eq:soft}. Massless external states are traded for conformal
primaries via the Mellin transform
\begin{equation}
\calO_{\Delta}(z,\bar z)
 = \int_0^\infty \dd\omega\, \omega^{\Delta-1}\, a(\omega, z, \bar z),
\label{eq:mellin}
\end{equation}
under which amplitudes transform as correlators of operators with
weights $h = \tfrac12(\Delta+J)$, $\bar h = \tfrac12(\Delta-J)$, and the
soft theorems sit at the conformally soft points $\Delta = 1, 0, -1,
\dots$ as poles in $\Delta$.

\section{A computation cell}

The $\Delta$-poles arise from the small-$\omega$ region of
\eqref{eq:mellin}. The basic integral, regulated by $e^{-\omega}$ (set
the scale to one), is the Euler representation of $\Gamma(\Delta)$,
whose poles at $\Delta = 0, -1, -2, \dots$ are exactly where the
conformally soft modes live. The cell below checks this on the local
Python kernel; its saved result is \nbresult{mellin-check}.

\begin{nb}[python]{mellin-check}
import sympy as sp
E, Delta = sp.symbols('E Delta', positive=True)
M = sp.integrate(E**(Delta - 1) * sp.exp(-E), (E, 0, sp.oo))
sp.simplify(M)
%out: $\Gamma(\Delta)$
\end{nb}

\section{A shadow integral}

Shadow transforms, $\widetilde\calO_{2-\Delta}(w,\bar w) \propto \int
\dd^2 z\, |z-w|^{2\Delta-4}\, \calO_\Delta(z,\bar z)$, map celestial
primaries to weight $2-\Delta$ and are needed, e.g., to build a
normalizable stress tensor from the subleading soft graviton. After
Schwinger parametrization the two-dimensional kernel integrals reduce
to one-dimensional pieces of the form below (Wolfram cell; needs an
activated local kernel).

\begin{nb}[wolfram]{shadow-integral}
Integrate[(1 + x^2)^(-Delta), {x, -Infinity, Infinity}, Assumptions -> Delta > 1/2]
%out: $\sqrt{\pi}\,\dfrac{\Gamma\!\left(\Delta-\tfrac12\right)}{\Gamma(\Delta)}$
\end{nb}

\section{Literature}

The infrared triangle --- soft theorems, asymptotic symmetries, and
memory effects --- is developed systematically in Strominger's lectures
\cite{Strominger:2017zoo}. The Mellin dictionary \eqref{eq:mellin}
recasting four-dimensional $S$-matrix elements as celestial-sphere
conformal correlators was set up by Pasterski, Shao, and Strominger
\cite{Pasterski:2016qvg}. For a self-contained review covering
conformally soft currents, the celestial stress tensor, and
$w_{1+\infty}$ structure, see Raclariu \cite{Raclariu:2021zjz}.
